# Lifting eventual periodicity to a global translation

## Intended lemma
Let $A\subseteq\{a_1,a_1+1,\dots\}$ be generated by the greedy gcd scan. If membership in $A$ is periodic beyond a threshold, then there is a positive $L$ such that
\[
m\in A\iff m+L\in A\qquad(m\ge a_1).
\]
Indeed, take $L$ to be a sufficiently large multiple of the tail period and of all finitely many relevant early accepted values. For an early accepted $m$, divisibility of $L$ by $m$ makes $m+L$ retain every prime divisor of $m$, so it is compatible with every accepted value. For an early rejected $m$, fix an earlier accepted witness $w_m$ coprime to $m$ and make $L$ divisible by $w_m$; then $w_m$ remains coprime to $m+L$. Tail values repeat by periodicity.

Once the accepted indicator has period $L$ on the whole half-line, each interval of length $L$ contains a constant positive number $T$ of accepted values and the ordered accepted sequence satisfies $a_{n+T}=a_n+L$ for all $n$.

## Status
The lifting argument is sound after carefully choosing the threshold and $L$; the missing ingredient is eventual periodicity.
