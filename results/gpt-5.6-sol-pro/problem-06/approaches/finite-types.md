# Projection to primes of the first term

Let $S$ be the finite set of primes dividing $a_1$. Every accepted integer is divisible by a prime in $S$. Thus its $S$-type is a nonempty subset of $S$.

For any $R\subseteq S$ which intersects every occurring $S$-type, an arbitrarily large integer supported exactly on $R$ is compatible with the whole accepted family and hence is accepted. Such $S$-smooth accepted values force every accepted support to meet $R$.

This provides finite combinatorial restrictions on the possible $S$-types. It does not immediately remove auxiliary primes: two accepted values with disjoint $S$-types can still be compatible through a prime outside $S$ (for example, types corresponding to $2$ and $3$ can be linked by the prime $5$).
