# Nonintegral-angle invariant

## Idea
Again normalize by $\theta$, with total angle sum $s=180^\circ/\theta$. For nonintegral $s$, Shan-Yu begins with an equilateral triangle; all three normalized angles $s/3$ are nonintegral.

Suppose a current triangle has three nonintegral normalized angles $a,b,c$. For a cut from the $a$-vertex, the two children are
\[
(b,x,s-b-x),\qquad(c,a-x,b+x).
\]
If both children had an integral angle, then, because $b,c$ are nonintegral, one of $x,s-b-x$ and one of $a-x,b+x$ would be integral. The four combinations respectively imply that $a,b,c$, or $s$ is integral. Every possibility is contradictory. Therefore at least one child again has three nonintegral angles, and Shan-Yu keeps it.

Since the target $1$ is integral, it can never appear.
