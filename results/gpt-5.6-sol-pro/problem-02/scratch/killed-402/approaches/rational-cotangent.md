# Rational cotangent-coordinate approach

## Idea
Set $AB=1$, put $A=0,B=1$ in the complex plane, and define
$$X=\cot x,\quad Y=\cot y,\quad Z=\cot z,$$
where $x=\angle KBA=\angle ACL$, $y=\angle LBK=\angle LNC$, $z=\angle LCK=\angle BMK$.
The midpoint conditions and the first equality give immediately
$$K=\frac{X+2Z+i}{2(X+Z)},\qquad L=\frac{X+2Y-i}{2(X+Y)}C.$$
Thus all quantities are rational in $X,Y,Z$ and the two Cartesian coordinates of $C$. The condition at $B$ is a linear equation in $C$, while the condition at $C$ is the constant-angle circle equation
$$|C|^2-C\cdot K=\cot(x+z)[K,C].$$
The plan is to show algebraically that, modulo the linear equation at $B$, this last equation is exactly the desired equality of powers of $M=B/2$ and $N=C/2$ with respect to $(AKL)$.

## Status
Promising and in progress. This avoids a large six-angle trigonometric identity and naturally explains the two possible configurations seen numerically.
