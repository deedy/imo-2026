## imo-2026-04

### Cut model (verified from statement)
P lies on the side opposite a vertex; cutting P to that vertex splits **that vertex's angle**, not the sides' angles. So if T has angles (P,Q,R) at vertices A,B,C and Mulan cuts from a point on side AB to the opposite vertex C, she splits angle R=∠C into r1+r2=R (r1,r2>0, chosen by Mulan, any reals). The two children are
  T1 = (P, r1, Q+r2),   T2 = (Q, r2, P+r1)
(sum check: P+r1+Q+r2 = P+Q+R = 180 ✓, and 180-P-r1 = Q+r2 exactly as the problem's "180° minus their sum" remark states). Shan-Yu picks which of T1,T2 survives. Mulan also does NOT choose the initial triangle — Shan-Yu does — so Mulan must have a strategy that works from **every** initial T.

### Distinct openings
1. **Shan-Yu invariant "all angles < θ" (proved rigorously, one direction of the answer).** If all of P,Q,R < θ and Mulan attacks R (>? actually R<θ too), T1 fails to keep the invariant iff r1≥θ or Q+r2≥θ; algebra shows: Mulan can force **both** T1 and T2 to violate "<θ" (i.e., break the invariant) iff there exist r1,r2>0 with r1+r2=R, r1≥θ-P, r2≥θ-Q — feasible iff (θ-P)+(θ-Q) ≤ R, which simplifies (using P+Q+R=180) to **2θ ≤ 180, i.e. θ≤90**, independent of P,Q,R. Hence:
   - If **θ>90°**: the invariant "all angles <θ" is *unbreakable* — for any triangle with all angles <θ (e.g. the equilateral triangle (60,60,60), or any acute/near‑degenerate one), whichever angle Mulan attacks, at least one child again has all angles <θ. Shan-Yu picks that starting triangle and always keeps that child forever: he never faces an angle =θ. **This is a complete, rigorous proof that Shan-Yu wins for all θ>90°.**
   - If **θ≤90°** (and θ>60° needed for the invariant to even be initially satisfiable): the invariant is breakable in one move — evidence this direction favors Mulan, but breaking the invariant only produces an angle ≥θ, not yet =θ; more work is needed to finish (see gap below).
2. **θ=90° exact universal 1-move win (proved rigorously).** For θ=90, given ANY triangle (no angle already 90), a triangle has at most one angle ≥90, so we can always choose which angle R to attack so that the other two angles P,Q are both <90. Set r1 = Q+R-θ (equivalently r2=θ-Q). Then T1=(P, r1, θ) contains θ directly, and T2 = (Q, θ-Q, P+r1) = (Q, θ-Q, 180-θ) — since θ=90, 180-θ=θ=90, so T2 ALSO contains θ. **Both children contain θ regardless of Shan-Yu's choice — Mulan wins in exactly one move, from any starting triangle.** This is a clean, fully rigorous special case.
3. **The "resonance" mechanism for general θ<90: create an angle =2θ, then halve it.** If some triangle in play has an angle R exactly equal to 2θ, splitting it as r1=r2=θ makes BOTH children (P,θ,Q+θ) and (Q,θ,P+θ) contain θ — instant win regardless of Shan-Yu. So Mulan's goal for θ<90 reduces to: force (in finitely many moves, against Shan-Yu) some triangle with an angle exactly 2θ. One move can often manufacture this: attacking angle R, set r1=2θ-P, giving T2=(Q,r2,P+r1)=(Q,r2,2θ) — an angle of exactly 2θ appears in T2. But Shan-Yu will simply discard T2 and keep T1=(P,2θ-P,180-2θ) UNLESS T1 also happens to contain θ. Direct algebra shows T1 contains θ automatically exactly when θ=60° (since then 180-2θ=θ) or θ=45° (180-2θ=2θ giving a second 2θ, not immediately a win but useful) — these are extra resonance points where Mulan wins in ≤2 moves universally, matching numerics. For a *generic* θ<90, this single 2θ-attack alone is not enough; Shan-Yu escapes to T1=(P,2θ-P,180-2θ), a triangle whose third angle is now the FIXED value 180-2θ (independent of the escape), a genuine invariant-breaking reduction of degrees of freedom. **Idea to escalate:** from this new triangle, if 180-2θ>θ (i.e. θ<60), Mulan can peel θ directly off this fixed leftover angle (general peel move: attacking angle X>θ with r1=θ forces one child to contain θ, the other survivor keeps a NEW leftover angle X−θ and permutes the other two). Iterating "peel θ off the leftover" repeatedly decreases the leftover by θ each round: 180−2θ, 180−3θ, 180−4θ, … — a strictly decreasing arithmetic sequence, eventually landing at a leftover ≤ θ, at which point the other two (surviving, adversarially-determined) angles necessarily sum to a multiple kθ of θ. **This chain (2θ-trap → repeated θ-peel on the leftover → forced multiple-of-θ sum) is the most promising concrete finite construction for general θ<90, but the final step (showing the two leftover angles, whatever Shan-Yu forced them to, can always be pinned to exactly θ) is NOT yet completed — this is the open gap for the outliner.**
4. **Naive "just keep peeling exactly θ" is a dead end (verified, do not retry as a whole strategy).** If Mulan always attacks by peeling exactly θ off whichever angle currently carries "+θ", the induced triangle map turns out to be **periodic with period 3**: (P,Q,R) → (Q,R−θ,P+θ) → (R−θ,P,Q+θ) → (P,Q,R). Shan-Yu, by always declining the branch containing θ, cycles back to the exact original triangle after 3 moves and survives forever under this strategy alone. Likewise the pure "peel-2θ" idea alone is periodic with period 2 (P → 2θ−P, fixed leftover 180−2θ). Neither naive single-move-type repeated strategy converges; a genuinely mixed/escalating strategy (opening 3 above) is needed.
