ALWAYS: state power-of-a-point computations in directed-length form (orient the line, keep AP signed); this removes all case analysis about which side of A the second intersection falls, and the reviewer flagged this exact issue in round 1 (round 1).
NEVER: report a ~1e-3 numeric near-miss as a theorem when the solver tolerance is 1e-13 (KL₂ = LK₂ looked plausible but is refuted); always compare the deviation against solver precision (round 1).
ALWAYS: for angle-chase geometry, encode hypotheses with unsigned angles of genuine triangles (law of sines, all sines positive) instead of Im(·)=0 complex ratios — positivity pins the branch, mirror components vanish, and the goal often becomes an unconditional polynomial certificate (imo-2026-02 solved this way, round 1).
ALWAYS: when a goal is a trig polynomial with angles appearing only as 2u, 2v, hunt the certificate as a bilinear identity in (cos2u,sin2u,cos2v,sin2v) via a sympy linear solve with affine cofactor ansatz; verify with rewrite(exp)+expand, NOT simplify() — simplify() can fail to reduce a true 4-variable trig identity to 0 while exp-expansion proves it exactly (round 1).
ALWAYS: make machine-found identities human-checkable by splitting into coefficient identities (by b², bc, c²; exploit b↔c,u↔v symmetry to halve the work) and displaying full product-to-sum Fourier tables with mode-by-mode cancellation (round 1).
NEVER: hand-transcribe signs into verification tables — regenerate each table directly from the certificate's coefficient collection in sympy first (a sign slip in 4 of 6 terms produced a bogus nonzero SUM before regeneration, round 1).
ALWAYS: before hunting an unconditional identity, check whether the goal only holds modulo the constraint variety — divide the exp-rewritten goal by the constraint-product deficiency with sympy cancel(); the quotient can be a one-term multiplier (sin(α−β) cracked imo-2026-02's KI, round 1).
ALWAYS: for circles through a fixed point, put that point at the origin and use the chord equation X cosθ + Y sinθ = |AP| from |P|² = 2⟨P,O⟩ — it eliminates all tangent–chord/power directed-length bookkeeping the outline flagged as a gap (round 1).
ALWAYS: prove multi-variable trig identities by interpolation: bound the frequency spectrum in one variable (e.g. odd, |k|≤3 ⇒ e^{−3is}·cubic in e^{2is}), evaluate at deg+1 points chosen to kill factors (s = t, 0, φ, μ−φ), Factor Theorem, then density+continuity — far shorter and more human-checkable than a 42-term expansion (round 1).
ALWAYS: eliminate an auxiliary angle u appearing in two constraints via the vanishing determinant of the homogeneous 2×2 system in (sin u, cos u) — division-free, branch-free (round 1).
