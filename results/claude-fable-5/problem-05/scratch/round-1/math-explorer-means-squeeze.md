## imo-2026-05

- Distinct openings (all under the means-squeeze lens):
  1. **The x=f(y) equality collapse.** Substituting x=f(y) makes both outer means degenerate to f(y) itself: QM(f(y),f(y))=f(y)=GM(f(y),f(y)), so the chain squeezes the middle term to equality on both sides simultaneously:
     f(y) ≥ (f(f(y))+y)/2 ≥ f(y) ⟹ **f(f(y)) = 2f(y) − y for all y>0.**
     This is the single cleanest algebraic consequence of the problem and should be the opening move of any approach built on this lens. (Verified symbolically/numerically below.)
  2. **Injectivity for free.** From f(f(y))=2f(y)−y: if f(a)=f(b) then f(f(a))=f(f(b)) ⟹ 2f(a)−a = 2f(b)−b ⟹ a=b (since f(a)=f(b)). So f is injective — no extra work needed, a direct corollary of opening 1.
  3. **Orbit/iteration argument forcing f(x) ≥ x.** Writing g(x):=f(x)−x, the relation f∘f = 2f−id makes the forward orbit of any y an arithmetic progression: y_n := f^{(n)}(y) satisfies y_n = y + n·g(y) (straightforward induction from y_{n+1}=2y_n−y_{n-1}). Since f:R_{>0}→R_{>0}, every y_n must stay positive for all n≥1. If g(y)<0 for some y, then y+n·g(y)<0 for n large enough — contradiction. **Hence g(y)≥0 for all y, i.e. f(x) ≥ x for all x>0.** (Numerically confirmed: c<0 in f(x)=x+c drives the orbit negative in finitely many steps.)
  4. **g constant on f-orbits.** From f(f(y))=2f(y)−y, rewrite as g(f(y)) = f(f(y))−f(y) = f(y)−y = g(y). So g is constant along the orbit {y, f(y), f(f(y)), …}. Combined with opening 3 this gives a nondecreasing-type rigidity but does NOT by itself force g constant across *different* orbits/starting points — that is the open gap (see below).
  5. **Swap symmetry x↔y** gives a second inequality chain B: sqrt((y²+f(x)²)/2) ≥ (f(y)+x)/2 ≥ sqrt(y f(x)), alongside the original chain A. Combining the AM-GM halves of A and B (adding them, or comparing them at specific points) is unexplored territory that could directly link g(x) and g(y) for independent x,y — this is the natural next substitution to close the "g constant everywhere" gap, since orbit iteration (opening 3/4) only controls g along a single orbit.
  6. **Sufficiency verification (the "these all work" half).** For f(x)=x+c with c≥0: since f(x)+y = x+c+y = x+f(y) exactly when f(x)−x is the same constant c as f(y)−y, the middle term (f(x)+y)/2 becomes literally AM(x,f(y)), collapsing the whole three-term chain to the textbook identity QM(x,f(y)) ≥ AM(x,f(y)) ≥ GM(x,f(y)), which is always true. So sufficiency for the whole family f(x)=x+c, c≥0, is a one-line algebraic identity, not casework — cheap to write up once the family is conjectured.
