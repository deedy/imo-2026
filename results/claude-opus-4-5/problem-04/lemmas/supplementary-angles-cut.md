# Supplementary Angles at Cut Point

## Statement

When a point P on edge BC of triangle ABC is connected to vertex A, the two angles at P (one in triangle ABP, one in triangle ACP) sum to exactly 180 degrees.

## Proof

Let t = angle BAP (the portion of angle A in triangle ABP). The angles at P are:
- In triangle ABP: angle APB = 180 - t - beta (where beta = angle ABC)
- In triangle ACP: angle APC = 180 - (alpha - t) - gamma = beta + t

Sum: (180 - t - beta) + (beta + t) = 180.

Geometrically: angles APB and APC are supplementary since B, P, C are collinear.
