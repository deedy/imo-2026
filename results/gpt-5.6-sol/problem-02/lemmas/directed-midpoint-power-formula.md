# Directed midpoint-power formula

Let a circle \(\omega\) pass through \(A\). Let an oriented line \(AB\), with \(AB=c>0\), meet \(\omega\) again at \(D=pB\) after coordinates are chosen with \(A=0\), \(B=c\), and let \(M=B/2\). Then
\[
\operatorname{Pow}_{\omega}(M)=\frac{c^2}{4}(1-2p).
\]
This remains valid when \(D\) lies on an extension of \(AB\).

Indeed, in directed coordinates on \(AB\),
\[
\overline{MA}=-\frac c2,\qquad \overline{MD}=c\left(p-\frac12\right).
\]
By the directed power-of-a-point theorem,
\[
\operatorname{Pow}_{\omega}(M)=\overline{MA}\,\overline{MD}
=\frac{c^2}{4}(1-2p).
\]
The same argument on \(AC\), with \(AC=b\), second intersection \(qC\), and midpoint \(N=C/2\), gives
\[
\operatorname{Pow}_{\omega}(N)=\frac{b^2}{4}(1-2q).
\]
