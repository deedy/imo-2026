# Circle second-intersection formulas through the origin

Let \(A=(0,0)\), \(K=(X,Y)\), and \(L=(U,V)\), where \(A,K,L\) are noncollinear. Put \(\Delta=XV-YU\ne0\). The circle through \(A,K,L\) has equation
\[
q_1^2+q_2^2-w_1q_1-w_2q_2=0,
\]
where substitution of \(K,L\) and Cramer's rule give
\[
w_1=\frac{(X^2+Y^2)V-(U^2+V^2)Y}{\Delta},\qquad
w_2=\frac{X(U^2+V^2)-U(X^2+Y^2)}{\Delta}.
\]
If \(B=(c,0)\), substitution of \(pB\) gives the nonzero-root parameter
\[
p=\frac{w_1}{c}.
\]
If \(C=b(\cos\alpha,\sin\alpha)\), substitution of \(qC\) gives
\[
q=\frac{w_1\cos\alpha+w_2\sin\alpha}{b}.
\]
Signed parameters cover intersections on line extensions.
