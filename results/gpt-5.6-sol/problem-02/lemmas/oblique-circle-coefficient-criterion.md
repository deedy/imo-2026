# Oblique circle-coefficient criterion

Let \(A=0\), take noncollinear vectors \(B,C\) as an oblique basis, and put \(|B|=c\), \(|C|=b\), and \(B\cdot C=bc\cos\alpha\). Write \(X=rB+sC\). Every circle through \(A\) has an equation
\[
c^2r^2+b^2s^2+2bc\cos\alpha\,rs-\lambda r-\mu s=0.
\]
Its centre is \(U/2\), where \(U\cdot(rB+sC)=\lambda r+\mu s\).
For \(M=B/2\) and \(N=C/2\),
\[
\operatorname{Pow}(M)=\frac{c^2}{4}-\frac\lambda2,\qquad
\operatorname{Pow}(N)=\frac{b^2}{4}-\frac\mu2.
\]
Thus
\[
OM=ON\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad \lambda-\mu=\frac{c^2-b^2}{2}.
\]
If the circle also passes through noncollinear \(K,L\), write \(K=r_KB+s_KC\), \(L=r_LB+s_LC\). Their substitutions determine \(\lambda,\mu\) in a nonsingular two-by-two system. Cramer's rule and determinant bilinearity give
\[
\lambda-\mu
=\frac{|K|^2[L,C-B]-|L|^2[K,C-B]}{[K,L]}.
\]
Therefore
\[
OM=ON\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
2\bigl(|K|^2[L,C-B]-|L|^2[K,C-B]\bigr)=(c^2-b^2)[K,L].
\]
