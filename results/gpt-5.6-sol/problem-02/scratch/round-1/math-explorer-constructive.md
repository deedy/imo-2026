## imo-2026-02
- Distinct openings:
  1. **Antipode-of-A local construction.** Let P be the antipode of A on circle (AKL). Then O is the midpoint of AP, while M−N=(B−C)/2. Consequently the desired equality is equivalent to PB=PC (equivalently, P lies on the perpendicular bisector of BC). This removes the circumcentre entirely. Moreover P is constructible locally as the intersection of the lines through K and L perpendicular to AK and AL. The three given angle equalities should then be read as instructions for rotations carrying the directions BK↔CL, the ordered pair BL/BK↔NL/NC, and CL/CK↔MB/MK. The hard step is to concatenate these local rotations or spiral similarities into PB=PC without silently assuming matching scale factors.
  2. **Equal-power / second-intersection encoding.** Since A,K,L lie on one circle, OM=ON is exactly Pow_(AKL)(M)=Pow_(AKL)(N). Let the circle meet AB,AC again at D,E. The target becomes the one-dimensional directed-product identity MA·MD=NA·NE. This is a promising cheap reduction: construct D,E from the angle data rather than locate O. Because M,N are midpoints, half-turns about them send A to B,C; this suggests seeking companion points/circles in which the midpoint factors become powers automatically. The hard step is discovering a direct synthetic description of D,E; a generic circle-intersection computation merely disguises a long trigonometric elimination.
  3. **Half-turn companions at the midpoints.** Apply the half-turns h_M:A↔B and h_N:A↔C to selected rays/points through M,N. The conditions at M,N then become ordinary angle conditions involving copies of K,L based at the vertices, while distances are preserved. A plausible local target is a pair of cyclic quadrilaterals exchanged by these half-turns, whose radical-axis or common-perpendicular-bisector statement is exactly the equal-power identity above. This framing uses the midpoint hypotheses structurally rather than as numerical factors. The warning is that independently reflecting K and L does not preserve the cross-conditions at B,C; one needs a single shared auxiliary circle or spiral centre.
  4. **Three-angle reconstruction algorithm.** Put x=∠KBA=∠ACL, y=∠LBK=∠LNC, and z=∠LCK=∠BMK. Then K is reconstructed from rays at B,M with angles x,z, and L from rays at C,N with angles x,y; the remaining incidences close a three-parameter rotation chain. This gives an algorithmic route: derive only the scale ratios of the associated spiral similarities and test whether their product is PB/PC=1. It is genuinely constructive if formulated as a composition of rotations/dilations; brute-force sine-law expansion is a fallback and likely becomes unwieldy.
- Candidate technique(s): Circumcircle antipode; equal powers; half-turns about midpoints; spiral similarity/rotation composition with scale tracking; radical axis; directed angles. The most promising top-level target is PB=PC for the antipode P, not a direct computation of O.
- Cheap-kill candidates: **Equal-power kill:** OM=ON iff Pow_(AKL)(M)=Pow_(AKL)(N), because both powers subtract the same circumradius squared. **Antipode kill:** if P is antipodal to A, then OM=ON iff PB=PC. Either reduction should be tried before coordinates or full trigonometry. No parity/counting-type kill applies.
