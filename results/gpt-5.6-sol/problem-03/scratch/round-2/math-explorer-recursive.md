Their difference is a signed sum with coefficients in `{-1,0,1}`. Interpret positive-coefficient parents and negative-coefficient parents as two piles and fold/overlay their cumulative interval partitions: equal overlap blocks become pairs of equal final pieces, with only the signed residual left unmatched. A zero-coefficient parent can be halved into its own equal pair. The cut budget matches sharply: if the signed support has `s` parents and there are `z=n+1-s` zero parents, overlaying the two supported piles costs at most `s-1` new cuts (including the truncation producing the residual), and halving the zero parents costs `z`, totaling at most `n`. This appears to be the missing structural reason for the denominator `2^(n+1)-1`; the outliner should verify endpoint/zero-residual degeneracies and distinct-mark legality.

This eliminates the need to control all order statistics in the upper-bound half and makes the folding construction insensitive to the order of the resulting pieces.
