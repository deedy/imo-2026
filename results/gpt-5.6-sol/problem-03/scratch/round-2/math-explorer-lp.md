## imo-2026-03
- Distinct openings:
  1. **Rank-word LP and its dual (lower bound).** Let Liu's parent-interval masses be `a_1,...,a_m` (`m<=n+1`). If Xiang's refinement has `q<=m+n` pieces, sort their lengths as `x_1>=...>=x_q>=0` and record the parent label of each rank as a word `w in {1,...,m}^q`. For fixed `w`, the closed-cell LP is
     `min sum_{j odd} x_j`, subject to `x_j>=x_{j+1}>=0` and `sum_{j:w_j=i}x_j=a_i` for every parent `i`.
     Every legal refinement lies in one such cell; conversely every strictly positive feasible point is physically realizable by cuts in the indicated parents. Zero coordinates and ties describe limits, which suffice for Xiang's infimum. A completely explicit dual form is available: with `c_j=1` for odd `j` and `0` for even `j`, it is enough to find parent prices `lambda_i` and nonnegative rank-boundary prices `mu_1,...,mu_{q-1}` (`mu_0=mu_q=0`) satisfying
     `lambda_{w_j}+mu_j-mu_{j-1} <= c_j` for every `j`; then `sum_i lambda_i a_i` is a certified lower bound for that word's odd-rank payoff. Thus Liu's dyadic guarantee can, in principle, be certified uniformly by a combinatorial rule assigning these prices to every word, rather than by optimizing cut positions.
  2. **Finite primal certificates and a polyhedral cover (upper bound).** For arbitrary `a` in the parent-mass simplex, Xiang's bound is not naturally certified by an LP dual (duals certify lower bounds on his minimum). The correct LP-side certificate is a *primal* rank word plus feasible ranked lengths with odd sum at most `C_n`. A uniform upper bound therefore asks for a finite, recursively describable cover of the simplex by polyhedra, each equipped with one such refinement. This route can prove both bounds within one rank-word language: dual prices on every cell for the dyadic point, primal witnesses covering all outer mass vectors for the obstruction.
  3. **Extremal characterization through active cells.** The candidate outer maximizer is, up to permutation, `a*=(2^n,2^(n-1),...,1)/(2^(n+1)-1)`. At this point a simple active cell halves every parent except the smallest: its ranked multiset is two copies of `2^(n-1),2^(n-2),...,1`, plus one extra `1`, and its odd-rank payoff is exactly `2^n/(2^(n+1)-1)`. In that active LP, exact dual parent prices are `lambda_i=1/2` on every halved parent and `lambda=1` on the uncut smallest parent; the active monotonicity prices alternate `1/2,0,1/2,0,...`. These certify the value of this particular cell exactly. The promising extremal target is the stronger characterization that any non-dyadic mass vector admits some different active cell with strictly smaller payoff; small cases support uniqueness up to permutation, but this is not certified for general `n`.
  4. **Rank-word compression by runs rather than enumeration.** Consecutive equal labels in a sorted word correspond to several children of one parent occupying a rank block. The LP constraints depend on the word only through label occurrences and rank-boundary incidence. A candidate uniform certificate should therefore classify boundary transitions/runs or recursively delete an adjacent odd-even rank pair, not enumerate all words (`8400` already arose at `n=3` under exactly `n` added children). This is a genuinely LP-combinatorial opening: seek a canonical reduction of any dual-feasible word to a shorter word while updating binary parent prices.

  `c_n = 2^n/(2^(n+1)-1)`.
  The claiming phase is eliminated exactly because each player, in this zero-sum common-value draft, must take a currently longest remaining piece; Liu therefore receives the odd sorted ranks.
