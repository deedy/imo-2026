## imo-2026-03
- Distinct openings:
  1. **Threshold-parity set under legal split toggles (best dyadic route).** For a final multiset of piece lengths, let
     \(E=\{t>0:\#\{\text{pieces of length at least }t\}\text{ is odd}\}\). The sorted alternating discrepancy is exactly \(|E|\), hence Liu's drafted amount is \((1+|E|)/2\). More specifically, if a current piece of length \(x\) is split into \(a\) and \(x-a\), with \(a\le x/2\), then the parity set is toggled by the symmetric difference with
     \[(0,a]\cup(x-a,x].\]
     Thus a legal mark is not an arbitrary parity edit: it flips two equal-length threshold bands at the bottom and top of one parent scale. This exact local rule is the strongest concrete opening found for a dyadic invariant.
  2. **Loaded profile theorem for Xiang's matching upper bound.** A promising whole-problem target is stronger than merely making \(|E|\) small: for arbitrary parent lengths \(a_1,\ldots,a_m\) of total \(S\), test whether at most \(m-1\) legal splits can make the odd-threshold set itself a single interval (or a nested interval profile) of length at most \(S/(2^m-1)\). This specializes at \(m=n+1,S=1\) to Xiang's required obstruction. It fits every computed sharp witness: for dyadic parents the residual profile is \((0,1]\) before normalization; for the round-1 \(n=3\) witness \((3,3,2,2,2,2,1)/15\), it is \((0,1/15]\). The interval-location data is likely essential for induction: a scalar claim about \(|E|\) throws away the cancellation needed when combining parent profiles. This is conjectural, not certified.
  3. **Dyadic lower bound as a minimum surviving parity band.** For Liu's unnormalized parents \(1,2,4,\ldots,2^n\), the needed statement is that after any at most \(n\) legal split toggles, the resulting odd-threshold set has measure at least \(1\). The exact identity \(2^k=(1+2+\cdots+2^{k-1})+1\) suggests that the indivisible unit is the strict geometric-sum excess, but the invariant should be formulated on the entire threshold profile, not on the number of pieces or on total mass. A potentially useful loaded version records the ordered components of \(E\) across bands \((2^{j-1},2^j]\), or a dyadically weighted boundary/frontier statistic, and asserts a one-unit residual after a budget of cuts.
  4. **Cancellation operations for the universal upper theorem.** Two elementary profile operations deserve testing as the cases of a stronger induction: halving a parent creates two identical children and cancels that parent's threshold contribution completely; splitting a larger parent \(x\) into \(b,x-b\) in the presence of another parent of length \(b\) cancels the two equal \(b\)-prefixes and leaves an effective parent \(x-b\). Scalar induction from these two operations alone does not cover all mass vectors, so it is not yet a solution; their value is that, with interval-position/profile information retained, they may become the two local reductions of the loaded theorem rather than an inadequate case split.
  5. **F2 interval algebra / boundary formulation.** Regard threshold profiles as finite unions of intervals with addition given by symmetric difference. Each original parent contributes a prefix \((0,a_i]\), and every split replaces one prefix by two prefixes, equivalently adding the two-band toggle above. Passing from sets to their ordered boundary points may turn the problem into cancellation of endpoints under a cut budget. This differs from the round-1 rank-word LP route: it keeps the legal ancestry of every split while discarding irrelevant physical positions on the stick.
