## imo-2026-03
- Distinct openings:
  1. **Finite rank-word/LP game.** Once the final lengths are fixed, optimal claiming is deterministic: sort them nonincreasingly and Liu takes ranks 1,3,5,…, so his payoff is the odd-rank sum. Encode each final ranked piece by the label of the original Liu interval containing it. A word on at most `2n+1` ranks, using each of Liu's interval labels, specifies which parent was refined into which ranked variables. For a fixed word and fixed initial interval-mass vector, Xiang's best refinement is a linear program: nonnegative ranked variables, monotonicity between consecutive ranks, and one mass-conservation equality per parent. Thus the continuous sequential game reduces to finitely many LPs (allowing zero variables gives the closure, and hence the correct infimum when distinct positive cuts are required). This is a whole-problem minimax formulation and is suitable for LP dual certificates.
  2. **Threshold/tail-count formulation.** The odd-rank payoff can be written using the distribution of final lengths above a threshold; the relevant parity is the number of pieces above that threshold. This suggests searching for an integrated parity/tail inequality attached to the original parent masses, rather than reasoning about Xiang's cut locations directly. This framing may produce a clean extremal inequality and is different from explicitly enumerating rank words.
  3. **Dyadic extremal/potential framing.** Computation strongly points to Liu's interval lengths being proportional to `2^n,2^(n-1),…,1`. Their total is `2^(n+1)-1`, and the conjectured value is exactly the largest interval. Candidate structural objects are binary weights, geometric-sum charging, or a potential that survives each adversarial split. This could avoid the exponential word enumeration while still addressing the whole game.
  4. **Adversary as refinement optimizer.** For the matching obstruction, view Xiang as redistributing each original mass among ordered rank slots under a global budget of `n` additional pieces. The active LP witnesses found for `n=1,2,3` are sparse and highly structured, suggesting a universal extremal refinement pattern rather than arbitrary cuts. A direct extremal characterization of such sparse LP optima is a plausible rival to the dyadic-invariant framing.
