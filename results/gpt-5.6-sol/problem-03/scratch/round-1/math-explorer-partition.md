## imo-2026-03
- Distinct openings:
  1. **Geometric interval partition / alternating discrepancy.** Once all marks are made, optimal drafting is forced by sorting the piece lengths: Liu Bang receives ranks 1,3,5,..., so his amount is \((1+D)/2\), where \(D=w_1-w_2+w_3-\cdots\) for the nonincreasing piece lengths. Thus the whole problem becomes a minimax question for the alternating discrepancy of a refinement of an interval partition. A strong candidate is for Liu Bang to make consecutive intervals proportional to \(1,2,4,\ldots,2^n\). The central lower-bound question is then: after at most \(n\) point insertions (each splitting one current interval), is the alternating discrepancy always at least one unit before normalization? This formulation is independent of the physical order of pieces after the cuts and looks like the cleanest whole-problem route.
  2. **Pairing/matching formulation.** For an even number of final pieces, \(D\) is the sum of differences in the adjacent sorted pairing \((w_1,w_2),(w_3,w_4),\ldots\); for an odd number, append a zero. One can instead seek a pairing of final pieces with controlled signed differences. For the dyadic initial lengths, each Xiang cut can disturb only one original interval, suggesting a matching between large and smaller pieces in which at most \(n\) dyadic levels are “damaged” and one unit of discrepancy survives. Hall's theorem is a plausible language if pieces are matched across size thresholds, though no Hall condition has yet been identified. For Xiang's upper bound, the rival target is a pairing/refinement of an arbitrary \((n+1)\)-interval partition with total paired imbalance at most \(1/(2^{n+1}-1)\).
  3. **Continuous polyhedral geometry.** Fix Liu Bang's marks and distribute Xiang's at most \(n\) marks among the initial intervals. On each cell determined by the order of all resulting piece lengths, \(D\) is linear in the Xiang coordinates. Hence minimizing \(D\) is a finite piecewise-linear optimization problem; candidate minima lie on chamber faces where two piece lengths become equal or a cut approaches an existing endpoint. This could turn Xiang's obstruction into an equalization construction or a compactness/limit argument. Distinct-mark restrictions matter: boundary minimizers may only be approached, but the game asks for the largest guaranteed lower bound, so an infimum can still determine \(c\). One must handle this quantifier carefully.
  4. **Threshold/integral geometry.** Since sorted alternating discrepancy can be read from the parity of the number of pieces exceeding a moving threshold (integrating a \(0/1\) parity function over the height variable), the dyadic claim may admit a scale-by-scale interpretation. The initial powers of two have nested threshold ranges; each split changes the parity profile in a controlled way. This is distinct from tracking the sorted list directly and may expose why one cut per dyadic level cannot erase the final unit.
