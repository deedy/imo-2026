## imo-2026-03
- Distinct openings:
  1. **Binary-splitting minimax abstraction.** Once Liu's marks are fixed, regard the resulting at most `n+1` interval lengths as heaps; every Xiang mark performs one binary split of one heap. The later draft depends only on the final multiset, not on positions. The apparent whole-problem core is therefore the minimax value of `n` adversarial splits followed by alternating selection from `2n+1` weights.
  2. **Pairing/discrepancy framing.** If the final lengths are `p_1 >= ... >= p_{2n+1}`, optimal drafting gives Liu `p_1+p_3+...+p_{2n+1}`. Equivalently his payoff is `(1+D)/2`, where `D=p_1-p_2+p_3-...+p_{2n+1}`. Xiang is trying to pair neighboring ranked pieces so that only a small unmatched residual remains; Liu is trying to make every sequence of at most `n` splits retain discrepancy. This reframes both bounds as one sharp discrepancy game rather than two unrelated marking tricks.
  3. **Threshold/layer-cake framing.** The alternating discrepancy has the identity `D = integral_0^infinity 1_{N(t) is odd} dt`, where `N(t)` is the number of final pieces of length at least `t`. Splitting changes this threshold count in a controlled way. This may expose the powers-of-two extremizer through parity across dyadic height bands, avoiding direct tracking of the complete sorted order.
  4. **Recursive minimax/folding framing.** Normalize the initial interval vector and study the value under one additional initial interval and one additional allowed split. The candidate geometric extremizer suggests a sharp recursion whose equality state appends an interval twice the previous total scale. This is a different top-level target from explicit neighbor-pair construction, though the needed minimax recurrence is not yet certified.
- Candidate technique(s): Backward induction/greedy optimality for the common-value draft; binary-splitting game; fixed pairing and unmatched-residual control; minimax recursion; layer-cake (tail-count) encoding of an alternating weighted sum; extremal principle and scaling. Candidate answer is
  `c_n = 2^n/(2^{n+1}-1) = 1/2 + 1/(2(2^{n+1}-1))`.
  The natural Liu marking has initial consecutive interval lengths proportional to `1,2,4,...,2^n`, i.e. marks at `(2^j-1)/(2^{n+1}-1)` for `j=1,...,n`.
- Cheap-kill candidates: First eliminate the drafting phase: with common valuations and no later choices affected except removal, backward induction gives the sorted odd-rank sum. Normalize by `A+B=1`, so only discrepancy `D=A-B` matters. Check whether Xiang can arrange equal-length adjacent pairs plus one residual; such a configuration immediately gives Liu `(1+r)/2`. Scaling and reversal of the stick remove irrelevant geometric degrees of freedom. Also distinguish exact forcing from an infimum: forbidden coincident marks can be handled only after checking whether the intended split lies strictly inside an interval or requires an epsilon perturbation.
