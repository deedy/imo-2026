- Distinct openings: **(1) Tropical/exponent-vector model:** let the finitely many primes occurring initially be coordinate axes and represent each board integer by its nonnegative valuation vector. A move becomes the coordinatewise transformation `(x,y) -> (min(x,y), |x-y|)`, i.e. overlap plus folded difference. This makes both termination and schedule-independence visible without comparing the integers themselves. **(2) Colored-stack/Ferrers geometry:** draw, for every prime, a stack of `v_p` colored cells at each position; a move removes the overlap of two stacks and transfers their nonoverlapping layers. Total cell count together with the number of nonempty positions is a discrete geometric potential, while the common step size (“content”) of each color’s stack heights survives. **(3) Divisibility-lattice/monomial transform:** replace each integer by the monomial `∏X_p^{v_p(n)}`; gcd is monomial meet and `lcm/gcd` is the relative symmetric difference. Total monomial degree supplies a rank, and the coordinate contents identify the sole terminal monomial. These are three presentations of the same strong transformation, but each gives a self-contained whole-problem framing far from size-extremal arguments on the original integers.
