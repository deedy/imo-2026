## Current best
For fixed \(x,y\), the hypothesis is equivalent to the simultaneous quadratic constraints
\[
(f(x)+y)^2\ge4xf(y),\qquad (f(x)+y)^2\le2(x^2+f(y)^2). \tag{1}
\]
Their two residuals have total width \(2(x-f(y))^2\); hence the feasible interval collapses when \(x=f(y)\), giving the exact iterate relation
\[
f(f(y))=2f(y)-y. \tag{2}
\]
This is the continuous-interval analogue of the two-substitution candidate-set collision in past problem `aimo-0186`: one should choose two independent comparison points whose quadratic feasible intervals for the same value \(f(t)\) have a unique common displacement. Unlike the orbit approach, the target is a local algebraic pinning lemma: for arbitrary \(a,b\), constraints generated using \(f(a),f(b),f(f(a)),f(f(b))\) should intersect only if \(f(a)-a=f(b)-b\).

Explicit gap: exhibit the two concrete substitutions and compute their interval intersection rigorously; an asymptotic or continuity-based collision is not allowed. Once the pinning lemma is obtained, positivity gives the common displacement \(c\ge0\), and direct substitution verifies \(f(x)=x+c\).
