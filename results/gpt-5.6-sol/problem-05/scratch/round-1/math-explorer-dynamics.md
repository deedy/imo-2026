## imo-2026-05
- Distinct openings:
  - **Reflected-map / inverse-dynamics framing.** Write the displacement as \(g(t)=f(t)-t\) and the reflected map as \(T(t)=2t-f(t)=t-g(t)\). Direct expansion of the two squared inequalities gives
    \[
    (T(x)-y)^2+4x\bigl(g(x)-g(y)\bigr)\ge0,
    \]
    \[
    (T(x)-y)^2-2\bigl(g(x)-g(y)\bigr)\bigl(y+f(y)+g(x)\bigr)\ge0.
    \]
    Hence, whenever \(T(x)>0\), substituting \(y=T(x)\) makes both square terms vanish; the remaining multiplier in the second relation is \(x+f(T(x))>0\), so the squeeze forces \(g(T(x))=g(x)\), equivalently \(f(T(x))=x\). Thus the whole characterization is very close to the dynamical statement \(f^{-1}=2\operatorname{id}-f\). If this holds globally, a two-sided orbit \(x_n=f^n(x)\) has zero second difference, hence is an arithmetic progression; positivity for every integer index strongly points to zero common difference and therefore the identity. **Exact hard gap:** prove the order bound \(f(x)<2x\) (or otherwise prove \(T(x)>0\)) for every \(x>0\), without presupposing surjectivity. This is the cleanest dynamics opening.
  - **Forward-orbit / ratio framing avoiding an inverse at first.** For \(x_n=f^n(x_0)\), the specialization \((x,y)=(x_n,x_{n+1})\) yields the verified orbit constraints
    \[
    x_{n+1}^2\ge x_nx_{n+2},\qquad x_n^2+x_{n+2}^2\ge2x_{n+1}^2.
    \]
    Thus the successive ratios \(x_{n+1}/x_n\) are non-increasing, while the RMS inequality prevents an arbitrary rapid drop when a ratio exceeds \(1\). More generally, using any two orbit indices gives
    \[
    2\sqrt{x_mx_{n+1}}\le x_{m+1}+x_n\le\sqrt{2(x_m^2+x_{n+1}^2)}.
    \]
    This is a genuine sequence-classification target rather than a pointwise inequality target. **Exact hard gap:** the adjacent constraints alone admit many plausible positive sequences, so one must exploit the full two-index family to force constant orbit increments or force an orbit to meet the region \(f(t)<2t\). Do not silently infer arithmeticity from log-concavity.
  - **Order-bound amplification from a hypothetical bad point.** If \(f(a)=A\ge2a\), the upper inequality at fixed \(x=a\) gives a global lower envelope
    \[
    f(y)^2\ge \frac{(A+y)^2}{2}-a^2.
    \]
    In particular this dominates an affine function \(\alpha y+B\) with \(\alpha=1/\sqrt2\) and some \(B>0\). Feeding any lower affine envelope \(f(y)\ge\alpha y+B\) into the lower inequality and optimizing in \(y\) amplifies its positive intercept on a tail. This offers a direct contradiction route against the universal \(\sqrt2\)-slope upper envelope obtained by fixing the other variable. **Exact hard gap / warning:** the constants are critical: at the natural optimization threshold, the \(1/\sqrt2\) lower slope and \(\sqrt2\) upper slope can meet exactly. A crude affine iteration may therefore never contradict anything; a successful version needs strict quantitative slack from \(A\ge2a\), or must separate \(A=2a\) from \(A>2a\).
  - **Fixed-point / extremal displacement framing.** The identity is the evident candidate, and \(g\) is the natural order parameter. The reflected-map formulas show that a point with \(T(x)>0\) copies its displacement to a preimage. One can ask for an extremal positive/negative displacement or an extremal ratio \(f(x)/x\), then propagate that extremality along this copied-displacement orbit. **Exact hard gap:** the domain is noncompact and no continuity or attainment is known, so maxima/minima of \(g\) or of the ratio cannot be assumed. Any extremal argument must use an infimum/supremum sequence and retain enough control to pass inequalities to a limit without assuming continuity.
