## imo-2026-05
- Distinct openings:
  1. **Zero-total-gap substitution and orbit rigidity.** After squaring the two valid positive inequalities, denote their nonnegative gaps by
     \(A=(f(x)+y)^2-4xf(y)\) and \(B=2(x^2+f(y)^2)-(f(x)+y)^2\). The exact identity \(A+B=2(x-f(y))^2\) makes \(x=f(y)\) a cheap equality-forcing substitution: both inequalities become equalities simultaneously, and the resulting orbit relation is \(f(f(y))+y=2f(y)\). This points to arithmetic-progression orbits and is the most promising whole-problem framing.
  2. **Displacement substitution.** Write \(f(t)=t+d(t)\). The orbit relation suggests \(d(f(t))=d(t)\), while positivity along a forward arithmetic orbit suggests \(d(t)\ge 0\). More importantly, the two squared gaps have fixed sum and their difference factors; nonnegativity therefore yields the sharp comparison
     \[
     |d(y)-d(x)|\,(2x+2y+d(x)+d(y))\le (x-y-d(y))^2.
     \]
     This turns functional classification into rigidity of a nonnegative orbit-invariant displacement. The natural strategic substitutions are points far out on two arithmetic orbits, with their values chosen so that \(x_n-f(y_m)\) stays bounded; then the right side stays bounded while the positive scale factor grows. The needed approximation is elementary nearest-integer/Archimedean approximation, not continuity.
  3. **Paired/swapped gap comparison.** Applying the condition to both \((x,y)\) and \((y,x)\) produces crossed factorizations such as \(B(x,y)-A(y,x)=(x+y-f(x)-f(y))(x-y+f(x)-f(y))\). This may offer a purely algebraic alternative to orbit approximation by forcing signs/equalities after ordering \(x,y,f(x),f(y)\). It is genuinely less developed and risks proliferating order cases, but could avoid an explicit approximation lemma.
  4. **Candidate-family reconnaissance.** Direct substitution of \(f(x)=x+c\) makes both squared gaps exactly \((x-y-c)^2\), so every \(c\ge0\) is a candidate. Testing the broader linear family \(f(x)=cx\) forces \(c=1\), consistent with translations rather than scalings. Best-guess characterization: \(f(x)=x+c\) for a constant \(c\ge0\).
- Candidate technique(s): Equality-case intersection in a chained inequality; squaring only after noting all quantities are positive; sum/difference of nonnegative gaps; strategic substitution \(x=f(y)\); displacement \(d(x)=f(x)-x\); arithmetic-orbit iteration; nearest-integer approximation between two arithmetic progressions; direct verification of the translation family. Needed facts for a builder to establish rigorously are: simultaneous equality at \(x=f(y)\), nonnegative and orbit-invariant displacement, and the global constancy of displacement from the factored comparison inequality.
- Cheap-kill candidates: The decisive cheap kill is \(x=f(y)\), because the sum of the two nonnegative squared gaps is then zero. Also compare the sum and difference of the two gaps before any expansion-heavy work. Positivity of all iterates prunes negative orbit increments. No injectivity/surjectivity assumption is needed at the reconnaissance stage.
