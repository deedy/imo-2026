## imo-2026-04
- Distinct openings:
  - **Affine angle-simplex game.** Forget side lengths and encode a triangle by the normalized angle vector `(x,y,z)∈R_{>0}^3`, `x+y+z=1` (one unit is `180°`). A cut from the vertex of angle `x`, splitting `x=u+v`, gives the two possible retained states `(y,u,z+v)` and `(z,v,y+u)`, up to permutation. Thus the geometry is exactly a continuous adversarial splitting game on the open 2-simplex. This is the clean algebraic framing and is genuinely far from manipulating the physical nested triangles.
  - **Backward winning-set / finite-rank operator.** For target `t=θ/180°`, the terminal set is the union of the three affine lines `x=t`, `y=t`, `z=t`. Define the predecessor operator `F(S)` to consist of states for which some split parameter `u` sends both possible children into `S`. Mulan has a bounded-time win from every state exactly when the simplex is exhausted by some finite iterate of `F` starting from the terminal set (an unbounded finite-time issue may require checking separately). This opens an algebraic classification by finite unions of affine strata, rather than a forward geometric strategy. Likely hard step: determine when the iterates fill the whole continuum and justify a uniform rank bound from pointwise finite victory.
  - **Denominator/divisibility invariant for the obstruction.** Rational-grid experiments strongly indicate the answer is `t=1/n`, i.e. `θ=180°/n`, for integers `n≥2`. For rational `t=p/q` in lowest terms with `p>1`, there are explicit losing grid states. The apparent obstruction is arithmetic: seek a residue-class coloring or homomorphism on angle triples modulo `t` (or modulo `1/q`) whose “bad” class is closed under Shan-Yu selecting one child. This would supply the necessity half without classifying all backward strata. Irrational `t` likely needs a separate affine/continuum invariant or a finite-tree algebraicity argument; merely reducing angles mod `t` does not by itself respect the fixed sum `1` cleanly.
  - **Extremal rank / Euclidean-algorithm flavor for sufficiency.** On rational discretizations, reciprocal targets `1/n` exhaust every state in only about `ceil(log_2 n)` predecessor layers (`n=2:1`, `3,4:2`, `5–8:3`, `9,10:4` in tested grids). This suggests a dyadic extremal quantity—possibly the number/size of target-length blocks in a partition of the angle sum—rather than induction on physical cuts. One-line opening only: encode each coordinate by quotient/remainder relative to `t` and search for a split making both children lower in a binary rank. Likely hard step: find a rank valid for arbitrary real coordinates, not only grid points.
  - **Finite decision-tree identity.** If a universal finite strategy exists, specialize it to a generic initial angle triple. Every node applies affine maps with one freely chosen split parameter, while every leaf enforces a coordinate equal to `t`. Because Shan-Yu controls the branch, all leaves of a finite binary subtree must hit those affine hyperplanes. Eliminating the cut parameters may force the constant sum `1` to be an integer multiple of `t`; this is a promising algebraic route to necessity and would immediately exclude irrational `t`. Likely hard step: Mulan’s cut choices can be arbitrary functions of the current real state, so one cannot casually claim polynomial/algebraic dependence without a careful finite-tree argument.
