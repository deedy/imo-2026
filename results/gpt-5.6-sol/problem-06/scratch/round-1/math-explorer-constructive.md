Once this finite-witness assertion is available, the accumulated conditions are a monotone Boolean state on the finite prime set `{p<=A}`: each old term contributes the requirement “the next residue is divisible by at least one prime in its small-prime kernel.” There are only finitely many distinct such requirements, so after finitely many terms no genuinely new requirement appears. Thereafter admissibility is membership in a fixed union of residue classes modulo the product (or lcm) of these finitely many primes; listing admissible integers increasingly gives `a_{n+T}=a_n+L`. The key unexplored point is the finite-witness assertion; a plausible contradiction opening is to choose the first pair whose common prime factors are all `>A` and use greedy minimality to manufacture a smaller admissible successor.   2. **Finite automaton from stabilized constraint types.** Instead of naming all primes `<=A`, seek a direct bounded “signature” of a term that records which previously established witness classes divide it. The family of constraints only grows, and duplicate signatures add nothing. If a bounded-signature lemma is obtained by induction from the least-choice rule, stabilization is immediate; the tail is then the ordered orbit of a fixed periodic predicate. This is materially constructive/dynamical rather than an algebraic classification of all possible prime-set families.
  3. **Minimal-counterexample contradiction to nonperiodicity.** Negating the conclusion means that the greedy rule creates infinitely many genuinely new obstruction types. Choose the first term creating a new type after all smaller types have appeared. Its novelty must be carried by a new large prime shared with some earlier term. Greedy minimality should then be challenged by replacing that large-prime witness with an already available small witness and finding an integer strictly between consecutive terms satisfying all old constraints. This is a direct contradiction target; the useful quantity is the first appearance of a new essential prime, not factorization of every term.
  4. **Induction on essential constraints, not on `n`.** Delete any old term whose divisibility requirement is implied by the other old terms. If one can show that a minimal antichain of essential requirements has bounded size (depending only on `a_1`) and that each replacement strictly lowers a finite lexicographic potential, the process eventually freezes. This opening avoids classifying the full pairwise-intersecting prime-support family and targets only constraints that can actually change the greedy choice.
