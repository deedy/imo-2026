  1. **Prime-support hypergraph / eventual periodic admissible set (recommended structural framing).** Replace each integer by its finite set of prime divisors. The generated supports form a pairwise-intersecting family, and at stage $n$ the eligible integers form
     $$E_n=\bigcap_{i\le n}\bigcup_{p\mid a_i}p\mathbb Z,$$
     a periodic set (period dividing $\operatorname{rad}(a_1\cdots a_n)$); $a_{n+1}$ is its least member above $a_n$. The actionable top-level target is not to guess the gaps directly, but to establish **finite stabilization of the constraints**: find a finite prefix $r$ such that every later term eligible for that prefix is automatically noncoprime to every later selected term, equivalently $E_n=E_r$ for all $n\ge r$. Then the sequence is simply the increasing enumeration of a fixed finite union of residue classes, and translating by a period gives the required $(T,L)$. The hard structural issue is exactly why the descending periodic sets $E_n$ stop shrinking; this route isolates the genuine theorem needed rather than presupposing a common prime.
  2. **Finite witness-type compression.** Every support intersects the finite support of $a_1$, so classify terms by their nonempty “type” $S_i=\operatorname{Supp}(a_i)\cap\operatorname{Supp}(a_1)$ (only finitely many types). Terms of intersecting types already satisfy the gcd condition through an $a_1$-prime. Only pairs of disjoint types need auxiliary primes. Explore whether, for each disjoint type-pair that actually recurs, greedy minimality forces one fixed auxiliary prime (or a finite auxiliary transversal) to become permanent. Since there are finitely many type-pairs, this would produce a finite prime set $Q$ capturing all future intersections and reduce the process to finitely many residues modulo $\prod_{q\in Q}q$. This is genuinely structural and distinct from manipulating the numerical gaps. Warning: merely taking $Q=\operatorname{Supp}(a_1)$ is false (seed 15 introduces the permanent prime 2).
  3. **Constraint redundancy / minimal antichain.** Divisibility gives redundancy: if $\operatorname{Supp}(a_i)\subseteq\operatorname{Supp}(a_j)$ then the condition imposed by $a_i$ is stronger, so $a_j$ may be discarded from the defining intersection for $E_n$. Track only inclusion-minimal prime supports (“active blockers”). Seek an extremal argument that the greedy process cannot create infinitely many genuinely new active blockers. A finite active antichain immediately yields a fixed periodic admissible set. This reframes the goal as termination of an online blocker process rather than periodicity of the original numbers.
  4. **Residue automaton after finite-prime capture.** Conditional on any proof that all relevant future gcd witnesses lie in a fixed finite prime set $Q$, encode a term solely by which primes of $Q$ divide it, or equivalently its residue modulo $M=\prod_{q\in Q}q$. The least-next-eligible operation on a fixed periodic subset is deterministic under translation by $M$; one traversal of its allowed residues supplies $T$ and $L=M$ (or a divisor/multiple). This is the clean final structural conversion and is analogous to finite-state recurrence arguments, but finite-prime capture must not be skipped.
