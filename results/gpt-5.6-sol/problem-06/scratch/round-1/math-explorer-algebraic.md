- Distinct openings: (1) **Boolean-CNF / divisor-antichain encoding (recommended actionable algebraic route, far from a structural graph framing).** For each term put `P_i={p prime:p|a_i}`. Admissible integers are the models of the monotone formula `F_n=∧_{i≤n}(∨_{p∈P_i}[p|x])`. Store this formula by its divisibility-minimal squarefree moduli `D_n`: `x` is admissible iff some `d∈D_n` divides `x`. The deterministic numerical update is `a_{n+1}=min_{d∈D_n} d(⌊a_n/d⌋+1)`, followed by `D_{n+1}=min_|( {lcm(d,p):d∈D_n,p|a_{n+1}} )`, where `min_|` deletes divisibility-dominated elements. If the increasing family of clauses has a finite basis (equivalently `D_n` stabilizes to finite `D`), then the *entire* sequence, not merely a tail, is the increasing enumeration above `a_1` of `⋃_{d∈D}dℕ`; membership is periodic modulo `L=lcm(D)`, immediately giving a translation. The actionable target for this route is therefore a finite-basis theorem for these greedily generated monotone clauses, preferably via minimal transversals/witnesses and the bounded-gap fact below. Computation strongly supports stabilization. (2) **Periodic-set / generating-function opening.** Define the limiting admissible set `S={x:gcd(x,a_i)>1 for every i}`. Independently of how a finite basis is obtained, prove the generated sequence is exactly the ordered enumeration of `S∩[a_1,∞)`: a skipped integer already failed an earlier clause and can never enter `S`. Thus it suffices to show `S` is a finite union of divisibility progressions. Then its indicator is periodic and its ordered enumeration has a translation; equivalently its rational generating series has denominator dividing `1-z^L`. This recasts the whole claim as semilinearity of a monotone divisibility language. (3) **Finite-state residue/transducer opening.** Once all minimal moduli are bounded, reduce the current value modulo their lcm; the pair `(D_n,a_n mod lcm(D_n))` evolves deterministically on a finite state space. A repeated state yields periodic gaps. This is analogous to finite-state modular reductions in recurrence problems, but the nontrivial issue is bounding/stabilizing `D_n`; do not invoke finite-state behavior before that. (4) **Prime-incidence matrix / tropical opening.** Regard rows as terms and columns as primes, with a candidate selecting columns that hit every previous row. `D_n` consists of inclusion-minimal weighted column covers, weight `∑log p`; the greedy successor is the least integer lattice point above `a_n` in the union of coordinate sublattices. Bounded inter-term gaps constrain how a large-prime column can recur. Search for a witness-count argument showing every essential column in a minimal cover lies in a fixed finite prime set, after which Boolean compactness is literal finiteness.
