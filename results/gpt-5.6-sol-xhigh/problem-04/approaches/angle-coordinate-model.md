# Angle-coordinate model

## Idea
A cevian cut can be represented using only the three angles; side lengths play no role.

## Status
Successful reduction, used in the final proof.

## Details
For a triangle with angles $(A,B,C)$, cut from the $A$-vertex and let $x$ be the part of $A$ adjacent to $B$. Every $x\in(0,A)$ is geometrically attainable. The children are
\[
(B,x,180^\circ-B-x),\qquad(C,A-x,B+x).
\]
In particular, the two new angles at the cut point are supplementary. This makes divisors of $180^\circ$ natural: if $180^\circ=n\theta$, arranging one cut-point angle to be $k\theta$ automatically makes the other $(n-k)\theta$.
