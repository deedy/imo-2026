# Tangent and homogeneous-vector factorization

## Idea
Use $p=\tan x$, $q=\tan y$, $s=\tan z$ after normalizing $A=0$, $B=2$. Replace rotation through $x+z$ by the homogeneous complex multiplier
\[
T=(1-ps)+i(p+s).
\]
The desired equality of powers is a determinant $D$, while the condition $C,K$ are on the required ray is the cross product $Q_T=[K-2N,TN]$. Exact elimination suggests
\[
(p+q)(p+s)D=p\{q+p(1+q^2)r\}Q_T,
\qquad r=t\cos x\cos y.
\]
