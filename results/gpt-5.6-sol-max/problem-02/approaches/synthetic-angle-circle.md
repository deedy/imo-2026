# Synthetic / angle-circle approach

## Goal
Seek a geometric interpretation of $OM=ON$, equivalently equal powers of $M,N$ to $(AKL)$.

## Initial observations
- Equal powers means the radical axis of the circumcircle $(AKL)$ and any circle centered appropriately lies along a line parallel/perpendicular to $MN$; more concretely, if $\omega=(AKL)$ then $\operatorname{Pow}_\omega(M)=\operatorname{Pow}_\omega(N)$.
- The angle equalities pair triangles $BMK$ and $CNL$: $\angle KBM=\angle LCN=x$, while $\angle BMK=\angle LCK=z$ and $\angle CNL=\angle LBK=y$. They do not directly make these triangles similar, but suggest spiral similarities involving $B,C,M,N,K,L$.
- Since $AM=MB$ and $AN=NC$, a power proof might follow by finding secants through $M,N$ to $(AKL)$ whose products reduce via the given angles.
