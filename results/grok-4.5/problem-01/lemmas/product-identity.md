# Lemma: product and coprimality identities of a move

## Statement
If $g=\gcd(m,n)$ and $\ell=\frac{\mathrm{lcm}(m,n)}{\gcd(m,n)}$, then
1. $g\cdot\ell=\mathrm{lcm}(m,n)$;
2. $g\cdot\ell=\frac{mn}{g}$, so the product of the two new numbers equals $\frac{mn}{\gcd(m,n)}$;
3. $\gcd(g,\ell)$ may be greater than 1 in general, but $v_p(g\cdot\ell)=v_p(m)+v_p(n)-v_p(g)$ for each $p$.

In particular the ordinary product of all numbers on the board is multiplied by the factor $\frac{1}{\gcd(m,n)}\le\frac12$ whenever $\gcd(m,n)>1$, and stays the same when $\gcd(m,n)=1$.

## Proof
Immediate from the definitions $mn=\gcd(m,n)\cdot\mathrm{lcm}(m,n)$ and $\ell=\mathrm{lcm}/g$.
