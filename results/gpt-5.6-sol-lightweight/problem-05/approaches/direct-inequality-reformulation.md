# Direct inequality reformulation

Writing \(f(t)=t+g(t)\), the original inequalities become
\[
-(\sqrt{x}-\sqrt{y+g(y)})^2\le g(x)-g(y)
\]
and
\[
g(x)-g(y)\le \sqrt{2(x^2+(y+g(y))^2)}-x-(y+g(y)).
\]
Both error terms vanish quadratically when \(x=f(y)\). This suggested trying to show that the displacement \(g\) is locally constant. On its own this asymmetric formulation did not immediately control all pairs, especially a hypothetical fixed point versus a point of positive displacement. The decisive improvement was first deriving the iterate identity and then setting \(x=f(u)\), which symmetrizes the comparison between \(f(u)\) and \(f(v)\).

**Status:** useful exploratory reformulation, superseded by the iteration-and-rigidity proof.
